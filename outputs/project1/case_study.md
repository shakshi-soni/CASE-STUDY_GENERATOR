# Problem
The small farmer cooperative in rural Maharashtra region faced challenges in accessing crucial information such as mandi prices, weather updates, crop disease treatment details, and government scheme information. This information was scattered across different sources and often outdated. Additionally, many farmers did not have smartphones with apps, exacerbating the issue.

# Approach
To address this problem, a WhatsApp-based agriculture bot was developed. This bot allowed farmers to message it and receive mandi prices, weather forecasts, crop disease and treatment suggestions, and government scheme alerts.

# Solution
The WhatsApp-based agriculture bot was built over a period of 2-3 weeks. It was then piloted with around 35-40 farmers across two villages.

# Results
The bot reportedly saved farmers time they used to spend traveling to check mandi prices, although no exact number was tracked on time saved. Some farmers also reported catching crop diseases early due to the bot's alerts, as mentioned anecdotally by the cooperative head. The adoption of the bot was slower among older farmers but faster among younger and literate ones.
